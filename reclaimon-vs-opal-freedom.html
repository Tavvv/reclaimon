ReclaimOn vs Opal, Freedom & one sec

Opal, Freedom and one sec are all excellent apps with loyal users. They also solve slightly different problems. Here's a fair, side-by-side look at how they differ — and where ReclaimOn takes a different path.

In short

Choose Freedom if you need one blocklist across phone, Mac and PC. Choose one sec if you want a mindful pause before opening an app. Choose Opal for scheduled focus sessions and gamified stats. Choose ReclaimOn if you want blocking that switches on by place and time, and progress that counts the minutes you put in.

Where they each shine

Freedom is the veteran of cross-platform blocking. If your distraction lives as much on your laptop as your phone, one synced blocklist across every device is genuinely useful, and hard to match.

one sec popularised the "mindful pause": take a breath, and often the urge to open the app is gone before the screen even loads. It's elegant and effective for reflexive pick-ups.

Opal leans into focus sessions and a polished dashboard of stats and streaks. If you're motivated by a focus score climbing, it's satisfying to use.

Where ReclaimOn is different

The others mostly answer "how do I block this app?" ReclaimOn answers a different question: "what am I trying to do instead, and when and where does it happen?"

1. It blocks by place as well as time. A Habit Anchor ties one goal to a real place and a real window. Arrive at home at 9pm and reading mode switches on by itself; leave, and it switches off. You don't start a session — the place and clock do.

2. It counts the time you put in, not the time you saved. Instead of a shrinking screen-time bar, the number on your home screen is the minutes you invested in a goal — climbing toward a weekly target.

3. It asks for honest feedback, not flattery. After a session it asks how it left you and how hard it was to stay. "Drained" and "Nearly quit" are valid answers, and they turn into insights about which anchors are set at the wrong hour.

4. It keeps everything on your device. No account, no server, no analytics. Read more on the privacy page.

Which should you pick?

If you split your day across laptop and phone, Freedom's reach is the deciding factor. If your problem is the reflexive unlock-and-scroll, one sec's pause is a clean fix. If a rising focus score keeps you going, Opal will feel good. And if your distraction is tied to specific moments — the evening you meant to read, the morning you meant to move — ReclaimOn's place-and-time anchors are built exactly for that, and they show you the life you're building instead of the feed you avoided.

FAQ

Is ReclaimOn a good Opal or Freedom alternative?

Yes — especially if you want blocking that starts automatically by place and time, and progress that measures the minutes you invest rather than the screen time you saved.

Does ReclaimOn block on Mac and Windows too?

No. ReclaimOn is focused on iPhone and iPad. If cross-device blocking is essential, Freedom is the stronger choice there.

The full app is free for 21 days with no card or account, then keeps a free-forever tier with 1 goal and 2 anchors. ReclaimOn+ is $5.99/month or $29.99/year.
